============================================================
BLOCKCHAIN PROBLEM STATEMENT 1 – RUZT-EZE SIMULATION LAB
============================================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Ruzt-eze Simulation Lab is a conceptual platform designed to act as a
“crash-testing laboratory” for DeFi protocols. Just like high-performance
race cars are tested under extreme conditions before being allowed on real
roads, DeFi protocols should be tested under adversarial and volatile
market scenarios before real-world deployment.

Current DeFi ecosystems often launch protocols without sufficient
stress-testing. This leads to liquidity drains, cascading liquidations,
stablecoin de-pegging, oracle manipulation, and exploit-based failures.
Additionally, most DeFi platforms are fully public and transparent,
making them vulnerable to MEV and front-running, while offering no
private or permissioned testing environments for institutional users.

The goal of PS1 is to build an AI-augmented, private or hybrid DeFi
risk and liquidity simulation platform that enables teams to simulate,
analyze, and audit protocol behavior before mainnet deployment.


============================================================
CORE OBJECTIVE
------------------------------------------------------------
Design and implement a platform that allows protocol designers to:

• Deploy permissioned or hybrid DeFi smart contracts
• Simulate extreme market stress conditions
• Model liquidity exits and liquidation cascades
• Run multi-agent adversarial market behavior
• Detect fraud and exploit patterns
• Generate measurable risk metrics and audit trails

This platform should function as a FINANCIAL INTELLIGENCE LAB,
not a production DeFi protocol.


============================================================
SYSTEM MENTAL MODEL
------------------------------------------------------------
The system consists of THREE CORE LAYERS:

1. DeFi Execution Layer (On-chain)
2. Market & Risk Simulation Engine (Off-chain)
3. Control, Analytics & Visualization Dashboard


============================================================
1. PRIVATE DEFI EXECUTION LAYER
------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose:
--------
To simulate institutional-grade DeFi environments with controlled access.

Key Concepts:
-------------
• Permissioned access (not fully public)
• Role-based execution
• Compliance-friendly structure

Implementation Requirements:
-----------------------------
• Smart contracts deployed on a public testnet (Sepolia)
• Role-based access using wallet whitelisting
• Identity-bound participation (simulated via roles)

Roles:
------
• Admin – deploys contracts, assigns roles
• Institutional Lender – provides liquidity
• Borrower – borrows against collateral
• Auditor – read-only risk observer

Important Note:
---------------
Privacy here means PERMISSIONED ACCESS, not private blockchain.
Only approved wallets can interact with protocol logic.


============================================================
2. LIQUIDITY POOL SIMULATION ENGINE
------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose:
--------
To evaluate AMM behavior under liquidity stress.

What is Simulated:
------------------
• Rapid liquidity withdrawals
• Large trade slippage
• Pool imbalance
• Volatility spikes
• Time-to-drain scenarios

Simulation Characteristics:
---------------------------
• Executed OFF-CHAIN
• Based on AMM mathematical models
• Driven by stress parameters

Key Metrics Generated:
---------------------
• Slippage curves
• Liquidity depth changes
• Impermanent loss exposure
• Time-to-drain measurement

Important:
----------
AMM math and stress modeling should be done in a simulation engine
(Python), not fully on-chain.


============================================================
3. LENDING & BORROWING PROTOCOL MODELING
------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose:
--------
To simulate how lending markets behave under adverse conditions.

What is Modeled:
----------------
• Collateral ratios
• Liquidation thresholds
• Borrowing caps
• Interest behavior (simplified)

Simulation Logic:
-----------------
• Track borrower collateral value
• Monitor price changes
• Detect under-collateralization
• Trigger partial or full liquidation

Expected Outputs:
-----------------
• Liquidation events
• Cascade effects
• Borrower risk exposure

Scope Control:
--------------
• Single collateral asset is sufficient
• Simple liquidation rules are acceptable
• Real oracles are NOT required (mock prices allowed)


============================================================
4. MULTI-AGENT MARKET SIMULATION
------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose:
--------
To simulate realistic market behavior using multiple actor types.

Agent Types:
------------
• Retail Trader – small volume, slow reactions
• Whale – large volume, aggressive moves
• Arbitrage Bot – fast, price-difference driven
• Liquidator – profit-driven liquidation agent
• MEV Actor – front-running / timing-based behavior

Implementation Approach:
------------------------
• Rule-based behavioral agents
• Configurable parameters:
  - Risk appetite
  - Reaction speed
  - Capital size

Important:
----------
Rule-based logic is acceptable and valid as “AI-driven behavioral modeling”
for hackathon-level implementations.


============================================================
5. DEFI FRAUD & ATTACK DETECTION
------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose:
--------
To identify exploit-like behavior before real-world exposure.

Attack Patterns to Detect:
--------------------------
• Flash loan attacks
• Wash trading
• Oracle manipulation
• Liquidity poisoning
• Coordinated pump-dump behavior

Detection Method:
-----------------
• Anomaly detection
• Rule-based alerts
• Threshold-based triggers

Example Alerts:
---------------
• Sudden borrow and repay in same block
• Abnormal volume spikes
• Rapid price deviation warnings


============================================================
6. DYNAMIC CREDIT & REPUTATION SCORING
------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose:
--------
To simulate institutional risk scoring.

What is Tracked:
----------------
• Repayment behavior
• Liquidation history
• Collateral management
• Exposure concentration

How Scores Are Used:
--------------------
• Adjust borrowing limits
• Modify interest rates
• Change collateral requirements

Implementation:
---------------
• Simple numerical credit score (e.g., 0–100)
• Updated in real-time during simulation


============================================================
7. RISK METRICS & AUDIT TRAILS
------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose:
--------
To provide measurable, explainable insights.

Metrics Examples:
-----------------
• Liquidity stress index
• Protocol stability score
• Liquidation cascade count
• Recovery time after shock

Audit Trail:
------------
• Smart contract events
• Simulation logs
• Timestamped actions

This data is critical for judge evaluation.
